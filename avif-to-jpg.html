Why convert AVIF to JPG?

AVIF (AV1 Image File Format) is one of the newest image formats, delivering exceptional compression — typically 40–50% smaller than JPEG at equivalent quality. However, it was only recently adopted by browsers and operating systems, and many apps still cannot open .avif files.

You will encounter AVIF files when downloading images saved by Google Chrome, Android cameras, or from websites that serve AVIF to supported browsers. When you try to open them in older apps, send them by email, upload to a portal, or print them, they often fail. Converting to JPG solves this instantly.

This tool uses your browser's native AVIF decoder (built into Chrome, Firefox, and Safari) to read the file, then re-encodes it as a standard JPEG. No plugins, no server — everything stays on your device.

AVIF to JPG, JPEG, or PNG?

JPG and JPEG are the same image format. The file extension can be .jpg or .jpeg; both refer to JPEG. This tool outputs .jpg because it is the shorter and most common extension.

Choose JPG/JPEG for photos, email, social uploads, document portals, and broad compatibility. Choose PNG only when you need transparency or crisp graphics, and expect larger files. If your goal is a smaller website image, consider converting the original to WebP instead.

Frequently asked questions

Why can't I open my AVIF file in Windows Photo Viewer?

Windows Photo Viewer does not support AVIF natively on older Windows versions. Windows 11 added partial support, but third-party apps may still fail. Converting to JPG fixes compatibility with all Windows versions and apps.

Will I lose quality converting AVIF to JPG?

At quality 90 (the default), the output JPG is visually indistinguishable from the original for most images. Set the quality slider to 100 for the highest possible fidelity.

Can I batch convert multiple AVIF files?

Yes — drag multiple files or use the file picker to select several at once. All are converted simultaneously, and a "Download All (ZIP)" button appears for bulk download.

Are my AVIF files private?

Yes. Conversion happens entirely in your browser — no file ever leaves your device. You can turn off Wi-Fi and the tool still works.
