Why image size matters for YouTube Community Posts

YouTube Community Posts appear in subscribers' feeds alongside text, polls, and video updates. Images that are too small appear pixelated or get cropped awkwardly; images that are too large are unnecessarily heavy and slow to load on mobile.

The recommended minimum for community post images is 1280×720 pixels (16:9 aspect ratio). This matches the standard HD resolution used for YouTube thumbnails, ensuring your image looks sharp on all screen sizes including 4K displays.

If your image is a portrait or square, YouTube will crop or letterbox it automatically. For best results, compose your images in 16:9 before uploading. Use this tool's "Contain" mode to add letterboxing, or "Cover" mode to crop the center to fit 16:9.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best image size for a YouTube Community Post?

1280×720 pixels (HD, 16:9 aspect ratio) is the recommended minimum. For sharper display on high-res screens, you can go up to 1920×1080 — the file size will be larger but the quality will be higher.

How many images can I add to one Community Post?

YouTube allows up to 5 images in a single Community Post. Each image can be up to 1MB. Use batch resize here to process all 5 images at once.

What file format should I use for YouTube images?

JPG for photos (smaller file size), PNG for graphics with text or transparency. YouTube does not accept WebP or AVIF for uploads — convert to JPG or PNG first.

Are my images private?

Yes. Resizing happens entirely in your browser using the Canvas API. No data is sent to any server.
