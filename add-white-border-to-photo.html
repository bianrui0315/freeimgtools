Why add a white border to photos?

Adding a white border or frame around a photo is a popular technique on Instagram and other visual platforms. The white space creates a "museum frame" effect that draws attention to the photo itself, makes dark-edged images stand out against the white feed background, and gives a clean, minimalist aesthetic.

White borders also solve a practical problem: square Instagram posts require 1:1 aspect ratio, but most photos are 3:2 (DSLR) or 4:3 (phone). Adding a white border lets you post any photo at its original proportions without cropping — the border fills the square format naturally.

This tool adds a uniform border to all four sides of your image using the Canvas API. The output dimensions are your original image size plus 2× the border size on each axis (e.g., a 1080×1080 photo with a 40px border becomes 1160×1160).

Frequently asked questions

What border size should I use for Instagram?

For a subtle frame on Instagram, 20–40px is common. For the classic thick border look, try 60–100px. Instagram posts are 1080px wide, so a 40px border on each side adds 80px total — bringing a 1080px image to 1160px, which Instagram will scale down cleanly.

Does adding a border increase the file size?

Slightly — the output image is larger in pixels (original size + border), so the file size increases proportionally. Use our image compressor after adding a border if you need a smaller file.

Can I use a custom border color?

Yes — click the color picker (the small colored square) to choose any custom color. Popular choices are white (#ffffff), black (#000000), cream (#f5f0e8), and pastel tones.

Are my photos private?

Yes. All processing happens locally in your browser — your photo is never uploaded to any server.
