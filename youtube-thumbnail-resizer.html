YouTube thumbnail requirements

YouTube recommends a 1280 x 720 pixel image with a 16:9 aspect ratio and a minimum width of 640 pixels. Its help page lists JPG, GIF, and PNG as accepted formats and a 2 MB limit for video thumbnails. These requirements can change, so check YouTube's official custom thumbnail documentation when preparing a high-stakes upload. Last checked June 23, 2026.

How to resize a YouTube thumbnail

  1. Upload your image.
  2. Choose 1280 x 720 or another thumbnail size.
  3. Download the resized thumbnail.

Crop without losing the subject

A square or portrait photo cannot fill a 16:9 canvas without a choice. Cover fills the thumbnail and crops the outer edges; move the important face, product, or text toward the center before resizing. Contain preserves the whole image but may add empty bands. Exact mode stretches the image and is rarely the best choice for a thumbnail.

  • Preview at a small phone size before publishing.
  • Keep text short and away from the bottom-right timestamp area.
  • Use JPG for photos and PNG for flat graphics or sharp text.

Fix a thumbnail that YouTube rejects

  1. Confirm the downloaded image opens and is JPG, GIF, or PNG.
  2. Use the 1280 x 720 preset so the result is 16:9.
  3. If the file is over 2 MB, run it through the image compressor and choose JPG or WebP-to-JPG conversion.
  4. Rename the file with ordinary letters, numbers, and hyphens if an unusual filename causes trouble.

FAQ

What is the recommended YouTube thumbnail size?

YouTube recommends 1280 x 720 pixels with a 16:9 aspect ratio and a minimum width of 640 pixels.

Why is my thumbnail too large to upload?

Video thumbnails should remain within YouTube's current file-size limit. Exporting a photo as JPG and lowering quality slightly usually reduces size without changing dimensions.

Should I crop or letterbox?

Use cover mode for a clean thumbnail crop. Use contain mode when every part of the original image must remain visible.
